MealMeld turns "What's for dinner?" from a one-person chore into a family vote. Instead of one person carrying the whole decision, the whole family swipes through candidate recipes and a consensus algorithm picks meals that actually make everyone happy.

### Why this app

Meal planning is stressful when one person carries the whole load. MealMeld distributes the decision across the family using ranked-choice voting. The Borda Count algorithm avoids polarizing picks -- it doesn't just find the most-loved option, it finds the option with the broadest support. Nobody gets stuck eating something they hate.

### How it works

Add recipes manually and create a voting session. Each family member swipes through the candidates -- Like, Dislike, OK, Super Like, or Veto (for allergies and hard constraints). The algorithm scores everything and surfaces the top picks. Schedule winners to specific days, and the app generates a shopping list organized by aisle with cost estimates.

### Privacy-first by design

No tracking. No analytics. No ads. All data is stored on-device with Apple's SwiftData framework. Sign in with Apple is offered but optional -- if you use it, only the Apple-issued user ID and a display name are stored, locally on your device. There is no server, no third-party account, and nothing leaves the device.
